How to do squats?


First:
Load a barbell on your traps and stand with your feet shoulder-width apart. Your gaze should be ahead, your chest should be proud, and your toes should be pointed slightly out. 
Second:
To set up, scoot your back down the bench with your knees bent and feet flat on the ground shoulder-width apart. Your legs should form a 90-degree angle, and the bench should be positioned right below your shoulder blade.
Third: 
Keep your chin tucked and drop your butt down toward the ground. Lower down while keeping your feet stationary, stopping once your torso forms a 45-degree angle with the ground.
Fourth:
Push up through your heels until your thighs are parallel to the floor again. Squeeze your glutes at the top, then return to the starting position.


How to single-leg deadlift?


First:

Hold a pair of dumbbells (as you are advanced, go with 15kg and add-up). Keep your back straight and your gaze straight throughout the movement.

Second:

Put your weight into your left leg and begin to hinge at your waist, keeping your left knee soft.

Third: 

Continue to hinge forward and lift your right leg up and back until your body forms a straight line from head to toe. Ensure that your hips stay square to the ground.

Fourth:

Pause, then return to the starting position and repeat. Complete the desired number of reps on each leg.

How to do RDLs?


First:


Hold a barbell or one dumbbell in each hand. Keep your back straight and your gaze straight throughout the movement.

Second:


Begin to hinge forward at your hips, lowering your weight toward the ground with a slight bend in your knees. Allow the weights to closely follow the line of your legs, and lower until you feel a stretch in your hamstrings.

Third: 

Continue to hinge forward and lift your right leg up and back until your body forms a straight line from head to toe. Ensure that your hips stay square to the ground.

Fourth:


Pause, then drive your hips forward to stand up, allowing your glutes to power the movement.
